How Larun Works

From raw telescope data to validated exoplanet candidates - a complete walkthrough of our detection and analysis pipeline.

The Complete Pipeline

1

Data Acquisition

Fetch light curves from NASA MAST archive. We support TESS, Kepler, and K2 mission data. Enter a TIC ID or TOI designation and we retrieve the relevant observations automatically.

2

Preprocessing

Clean and normalize the data. Remove outliers, correct for systematic effects, and detrend stellar variability. This prepares the light curve for transit detection.

3

BLS Analysis

Box Least Squares periodogram searches for periodic box-shaped dips in brightness. This classical algorithm identifies potential transit periods with high sensitivity.

4

TinyML Detection

Our neural network models confirm and classify detected signals. The EXOPLANET-001 model distinguishes real transits from false positives like eclipsing binaries.

5

Parameter Fitting

Fit a transit model to derive planetary parameters: orbital period, transit depth, duration, and estimated planet radius. Uncertainties are calculated using MCMC sampling.

6

Vetting & Validation

Calculate False Positive Probability (FPP) and check for common false positive scenarios. Generate disposition: VALIDATED, CANDIDATE, or LIKELY_FP.

What Makes Larun Different

Browser-Based: All processing happens locally in your browser using TensorFlow.js. No server uploads required - your data stays private.

TinyML Models: Our models are under 100KB each, enabling instant inference without GPU requirements.

Real NASA Data: Direct integration with NASA's MAST archive means you're analyzing real telescope observations.
